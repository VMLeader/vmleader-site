About Vertical Motion

We've sat in the seats
we coach.

Vertical Motion is a team of former and current CEOs, military officers, operators, and coaches who've lived the career and leadership challenges that keep good people stuck. While we understand the theory, we mix data analysis with intuition and experience.

Our why

We help good people become great leaders.

Vertical Motion was founded in early 2023, while Tom Cox was still fighting a battle against Acute Myeloid Leukemia. When he rang the bell and was given a new lease on life, Tom retired from a 30-year career in Aerospace and Defense and decided to devote the rest of his life to one thing: helping good people become great leaders.

Leukemia taught Tom how much it matters to believe in what you do. That is why our programs are different. We help you find yourself first, then decide where to go next.

We only work with good people, because we believe the world needs better leaders and we exist to develop them: the authentic, empathetic, and audacious leaders who lift everyone around them.

Authentic

Lead as yourself, not from behind a mask. Your real self is your edge.

Empathetic

Great leaders lift the people around them, not just their own results.

Audacious

Bold enough to go after what you are truly capable of, and to help others do the same.

Founder's Story

Tom Cox

Founder • Executive Coach • Author • 3x CEO • Army Veteran • Leukemia Survivor

Tom is the Founder & CEO of Vertical Motion, which turns good people into great leaders. He works with CEOs to build, fix, and scale their executive leadership teams (ELTs), and he also works with aspiring executives to step into VP/C-Suite roles by leading with authenticity, empathy, and audacity.

Tom is an executive advisor & coach, a published author, a US Army veteran, a 2x VC-backed founder, and a seasoned sales leader who has closed over $1.5B in contracts throughout his distinguished 30-year career in Aerospace & Tech. He has an MBA from Georgia State University.

A passionate advocate for helping good people become better versions of themselves, his mission is to empower the next generation to lead with authenticity, empathy, and audacity – traits necessary to lead effectively in the mid-21st century.

RR

Ryan Roberts

Executive Coach • Head of Operations & Client Success • 3x CEO • Air Force Officer & Fighter Pilot

Ryan served 20 years as a U.S. Air Force officer and fighter pilot, leading high-stakes missions and shaping military strategy at the highest levels. After transitioning from the Air Force, he built and ran successful businesses, applying his expertise in leadership, strategy, and execution to drive measurable growth. As co-founder of a defense contracting firm, he secured multimillion-dollar contracts and led initiatives that set new safety standards in military training.

In 2018, Ryan embraced an unconventional lifestyle, leading his company remotely while living aboard a sailboat and exploring over a dozen countries and covering over 10,000 miles in the Atlantic and Caribbean. This experience reinforced his belief in adaptability, strategic vision, and the power of bold decision-making in uncertain environments.

Today, Ryan is responsible for overseeing business operations and ensuring client success, while also mentoring and coaching leaders to turn ambitious visions into reality. With extensive experience in executive leadership and strategic execution, he guides professionals in refining their leadership style, improving operations, and scaling businesses effectively. He emphasizes the importance of resilience and high performance, helping leaders thrive under pressure and navigate conflict as a driver for growth.

Ryan is committed to building high-performing teams and fostering a culture of continuous growth and innovation. He believes that the right combination of strategy, execution, and adaptability empowers leaders to achieve lasting impact in their organizations and beyond.

CM

Chris Martin

Vertical Motion Certified Coach

Chris came to leadership through an unlikely door. He graduated with a fine arts degree; an unexpected opportunity landed him in wealth management, and he never looked back. The fine arts background gave him a different lens than most of his peers, a habit of noticing what others walked past. That perspective never left him. Neither did a core belief: that people are almost always more capable than they know.

Over the next 20 years, Chris worked inside organizations at nearly every scale, from a mid-size wealth management firm to a Fortune 500 brokerage to a high-growth fintech. What he kept seeing, regardless of size, was the same pattern: organizations drift as a natural consequence of growth. The same conversations resurface in slightly different form. Nothing is obviously broken, but progress slows anyway.

That outside perspective is what Chris brings today. He works with founders and emerging leaders who are ready for an honest outside read, someone who can see the system clearly, name what's actually happening, and help them reframe their situation so they can move forward with confidence. Chris holds an MBA from the University of Denver and a Project Management Professional (PMP) certification. He is based in North Vancouver, BC.
