What got you here will not get you there

You are doing everything right, and still getting passed over.

If you are a high performing mid-career professional who feels stuck below the executive line, you are not short on talent. You are playing the wrong game.

"I work harder than anyone, and watch others get promoted."

Hard work earned you a plaque and a steak dinner, not the title. The executive game runs on different rules than the one that got you here.

"I submit resumes and hear nothing back."

It feels like shouting into the void. Up to 90% of executive roles are never posted, so the resume game was rigged against you from the start.

"I refuse to play political games to get ahead."

Good. There are two ways up: manipulation and leadership. This book shows you how the quiet good leaders win without the games.

Why I wrote this

I spent 15 years learning what nobody tells you.

In 2007 I was a mid-career professional working hundred-hour weeks, convinced that effort alone would earn the promotion. It did not. So I spent $100K and two years on an MBA. My boss told me plainly that it would not get me promoted. He was right.

After three years of waiting for "good things" to come, I built my own company instead, and learned the hard way what it takes to lead at the top. Over the next 15 years I figured out what actually works, and why so many talented people get stuck.

This is the handbook I wish someone had handed me the day I decided to move up.

Inside, I share the exact process my team and I have used to coach hundreds of professionals through the glass ceiling and into Director, VP, and C-suite roles.
